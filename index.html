Weather Station Insights

My personal weather station’s take on local climate, from pool temps to lightning strikes

Microclimate Dynamics

Ever wonder how my pool and garage compare to the outdoor air? This shows the average temperature difference in Fahrenheit (°F). The pool’s warmer in summer due to sunlight, while the garage stays cozy year-round thanks to insulation. The graph tracks monthly pool differences from May to September—when I use it!

Pool Average Difference: 5.61°F | Garage Average Difference: 23.81°F

Soil Moisture Response

After it rains, how does my garden soil soak it up? ‘Lag’ is how many hours it takes for moisture to hit its peak—think of it as the soil drinking up water. ‘Decay’ is how fast it dries out afterward (in 1/hour). These numbers help me know when to water my plants!

Average Lag Time: 2.17 hours | Drying Rate: 0.001 per hour

Lightning Activity

Storms can feel close, and this shows how often lightning strikes near my home. The pie chart breaks it down by distance in miles—‘<10 mi’ means really close! It counts strikes over a year, giving a sense of how stormy my area gets.

Strikes Within 10 Miles: 18946

Air Quality (PM2.5)

PM2.5 measures tiny air particles (in micrograms per cubic meter, µg/m³)—lower is better for breathing! I compare rainy vs. dry days to see if rain washes them away. These levels tell me about local air health.

Rainy Days: 8.16 µg/m³ | Dry Days: 8.34 µg/m³

Wind Patterns

Which way does the wind blow around here? The wind rose shows direction (like a compass: 0° is north, 90° east) and speed (in miles per hour, mph). ‘Persistence’ is how long it stays steady before shifting—short times mean gusty days!

Average Steady Time: 0.17 hours

Energy Balance

This estimates how much heat energy (in watts, W) my pool gains, mostly from the sun. It’s highest in summer when sunlight’s strong. The graph shows monthly averages—great for understanding pool heating patterns!
